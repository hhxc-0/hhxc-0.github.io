Who am I?

I'm a student exploring embodied AI and robotics to build agentic systems for autonomous scientific discovery.

Broadly, I work at the intersection of robotics, reinforcement learning, and multi-agent reasoning to bridge the physical-digital gap and advance safe, ethically aligned AI.

Specifically, I'm focused on closing the reliability gap—improving uncertainty quantification so agents know what they don't know—through deterministic safety guarantees, causal-mechanistic architectures, and adversarial validation.

While woking at AIXC

I attended IJCAI 2023 and 2024 and presented my poster, “The Ascendant AI Scientist,” at the IEEE IROS 2025 AIR4S workshop. Next, I'm extending our adversarial validation framework to higher-dimensional PDEs, deepening the theory behind the correlation-calibration trade-off in uncertainty quantification, releasing a reproducible codebase, and revising work on verified actuation. I'll also pursue submissions on causal-inference-grounded agent architectures with deterministic safety guarantees, safe exploration in open-world RL, intelligent control for wearable exoskeletons, and multi-agent voice-first interactions.
